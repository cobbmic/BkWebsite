How will bankruptcy affect my credit score?

Bankruptcy has varying effects on credit scores, depending on the individual and his or her credit history. Some credit scores may improve while others will be lowered by a bankruptcy filing. We can't estimate how your credit score will be affected by your bankruptcy score.

How will bankruptcy affect the credit scores of my co-signers?

The credit scores of your cosigners may be affected by your bankruptcy case. This will vary depending on individual factors. We can't estimate how your credit score or the credit score of your cosigners will be affected by your bankruptcy filing.

I have a bankruptcy case number. Why is my credit report still not cleared up?

Credit reports will NOT be fully updated until 3-6 months AFTER your bankruptcy is over. If you see something on your credit report that looks wrong, please contact the credit bureau to dispute it. Our office has no relationship with credit bureaus and cannot address credit report errors.

How do I pay my $335 filing fee?

To pay the $335 filing fee, MAIL a money order for at least $83.75 each month, made out to Cobb Law Firm, LLC and addressed to Cobb Law Firm, P. O. Box 306, Anniston, AL 36202. What do I do if one of my vehicles is in danger of repossession?

If a vehicle is in danger of repossession, NOTIFY the creditor of the bankruptcy.

When will I find out my court date?

Within TWO WEEKS, the court will mail you a court date. You can see an example of the form you will receive by clicking this link: 341 Notice -- Ch. 7.

When will I receive a copy of my bankruptcy paperwork?

You will receive a copy of your bankruptcy paperwork at the first hearing.

What do I do if one of my creditors calls?

If any creditor calls, give them your bankruptcy case number.

What should I do about any property I want to keep?

Keep your payments current on the property you want to keep.

What should I do if my phone number changes or if I move?

Keep the office of Cobb Law Firm informed of good phone number(s) and address(es).

What should I do about liens against me?

It is your responsibility to get us copies of liens recorded against you in the Probate Office.

When should I complete the DVD credit counseling course?

Complete your DVD credit counseling as SOON as you get a case order. Not before! DO NOT complete any other course than the one we give you.

Will the credit score of my co-signers be affected by my bankruptcy?

The credit score of your co-signers may be lowered by your bankruptcy.

How long will my bankruptcy be on my credit report?

Your bankruptcy may be on your credit report for ten (10) years.

What should I do with property I do not plan to keep?

You have 30 days from the date of your first scheduled hearing to surrender unwanted property.

What debts will Chapter 7 not get rid of (discharge)?

Chapter 7 will NOT get rid of (discharge): most taxes, student loans, child support, alimony, criminal debts, debts made through fraud, most retirement loans, and any other debts that the bankruptcy laws will not let be discharged.

How will bankruptcy affect the credit scores of my co-signers?

The credit scores of your cosigners may be affected by your bankruptcy case. This will vary depending on individual factors. We can't estimate how your credit score or the credit score of your cosigners will be affected by your bankruptcy filing.

I have a bankruptcy case number. Why is my credit report still not cleared up?

Credit reports will NOT be fully updated until 3-6 months AFTER your bankruptcy is over. If you see something on your credit report that looks wrong, please contact the credit bureau to dispute it. Our office has no relationship with credit bureaus and cannot address credit report errors.

What do I do if I am behind on car payments?

If you are behind on car payments, please notify the creditor of your Chapter 13.

What sohuld I do if one of my creditors calls me?

If any creditor calls you, give them your Chapter 13 case number and the number of Cobb Law Firm (256-237-2250).

How can I reach the Cobb Law Firm?

The Cobb Law Firm, LLC, can be reached by telephone (866-626-9245), email (cobblawfirm@gmail.com), fax (800-850-5731), and mail (P. O. Box 306, Anniston, AL 36202).

When is my court date?

You will receive court dates in the mail within 2 weeks. You can see an example of the form here: 341 Notice -- Ch. 13.

When will I receive a copy of my Chapter 13 Petition?

You will receive a copy of your Chapter 13 Petition at your first court hearing.

What kind of house and car insurance should I have?

Make sure that you keep full coverage insurance on your cars and home that are in Chapter 13.

What debts will I still owe after my Chapter 13 ends?

After your Chapter 13 ends, you will still owe all these that are unpaid: taxes, student loans, criminal debts, debts incurred through fraud, domestic obligation, interest on child support, post-petition debts or mortgage fees, debts not listed, long-term debts, all debts from another bankruptcy filed less than four (4) years ago and liens.

What about liens filed at the Probate Court?

It is your responsibility to give us a copy of all liens filed at the Probate Court.

What if my address or phone number changes?

Keep your address and phone number updated while in Chapter 13. Please inform the office of Cobb Law Firm if either changes.

Can I take out new loans or get into more debt while in my Chapter 13?

No new debts, except medical bills, are allowed without prior court approval.

What credit counseling course should I complete?

You have been furnished with a DVD and booklet to complete within 2 weeks AFTER you get your case number. Do NOT complete any other course than the one we give you.

What about my house payment within Chapter 13?

You must pay every payment when due.

Where do I make my Chapter 13 payment?

If it is not payroll deducted, make your payment online at www.tfsbillpay.com or by phone at 1-888-729-2413. You can also send a money order to Linda B. Gore, POB 205, Gadsden, AL 35902.

Should I keep paying my debts?

Do not pay any more of your debts, unless your Chapter 13 Plan so provides.

I just received an "Objection to Confirmation and Motion to Dismiss" letter. What do I do? Is this bad?

Everyone will receive an "Objection to Confirmation and Motion to Dismiss" from the Trustee before your 2nd Court Hearing. Don't panic and don't call! Everyone gets this notice.

What should I do if I get a pay increase?

During your bankruptcy, if you get a pay raise that is greater than a cost-of-living increase, please send us proof of income.
